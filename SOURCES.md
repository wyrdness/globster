## Notes on Sources

### Reliability Assessment

**High Reliability**:
- Peer-reviewed scientific journals (DNA studies, histology)
- University research publications
- Museum specimen records

**Medium Reliability**:
- Historical newspaper accounts (contemporary to events)
- Published books by credentialed researchers
- Expert interviews

**Lower Reliability** (contextual use only):
- Internet forums and discussion boards
- Unverified eyewitness accounts
- Sensationalist media coverage

### Primary vs. Secondary Sources

**Primary Sources**:
- DNA analysis papers (Pierce et al., 2004)
- Historical examination reports (Verrill, 1897)
- Original photographs and specimens

**Secondary Sources**:
- Books synthesizing multiple cases
- Documentary programs
- Review articles
